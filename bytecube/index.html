Good to know

Frequently asked questions

What is ByteCube?

ByteCube is a 3D version of the merge puzzle 2048. Instead of a flat grid, the numbers live on a rotating cube. You spin the cube to any angle, tap a face, and every tile slides inward — matching numbers double. It's simple to start and hard to put down, with a new daily puzzle and cube sizes from 3³ up to 10³.

Is ByteCube free to play?

Yes. You can play a free 3³ demo right now in your browser, and the iPhone app (coming soon) is free as well. The iPhone app is supported by ads, with an optional Remove Ads subscription for $0.99/month — and completing the daily challenge seven days in a row unlocks seven days ad-free for free.

How do you play ByteCube?

Drag to rotate the cube, then tap one of its faces. Every tile on the cube slides toward that face, and any two equal numbers that collide merge into their double. Keep merging to reach 2,048 and beyond. The board fills as you play, so the game ends when no moves are left — the real challenge is choosing which face to push next.

What are wild and spawner tiles?

Wild tiles merge with any number, giving you a way out of a stuck board. Spawner tiles, which appear on 5³ cubes and larger, merge normally but drop one extra block onto the cube — they help and hurt at the same time, raising the density and the risk.

How does the daily challenge work?

Every day ByteCube generates one deterministic cube — the same size and the same seed for every player in the world — so you're all solving the identical puzzle. It resets at your local midnight, anchored to server time so changing your device clock won't unlock the next one early. Chase your best and climb the daily leaderboard.

Do I need an account or an internet connection?

No account is required and every cube size is fully playable offline. Your best scores are stored on your device. The only time ByteCube goes online is to show the optional worldwide leaderboard, where you can post a self-chosen display name with your score.
