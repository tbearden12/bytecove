01 What AI Can Actually Do For You

Not magic. Better than the group text.

Most of what we build looks boring on a slide. It's the boring parts that return hours back to your week. Here are the kinds of problems we take on — and what the “after” looks like once it's running.

A

The inbox that everyone forwards to. Customer emails, bid requests, vendor questions — all landing in one place, sorted by nobody. Emails are read, tagged, and routed to the right person. Urgent ones bubble up. Quotes get a draft reply within an hour.

B

The spreadsheet nobody trusts. Three people edit it, two people never open it, and nobody is sure which tab is current. One dashboard. One source of truth. Fields that can't be typed wrong.

C

The report that takes half a day. Someone exports to CSV, pastes into a template, fixes the numbers, emails it out — every Monday. The report writes itself and lands in your inbox at 7am. Numbers tie because they're pulled straight from the system.

D

The quote that takes forty minutes. Look up the customer. Pull last year's numbers. Crosswalk the part list against a PDF catalog. Hope you didn't miss a line item. A form, a few dropdowns, a PDF out the other side. Priced from live data. Three minutes.

E

The call you only knew about when the customer got mad. No record of who said what, no paper trail, no follow-up reminder. Every call summarized, searchable, and tied to the job. Follow-ups set automatically.

Four ways we show up.

Every engagement is different, but most land inside one of these four buckets. We'll tell you straight whether what you need is one of them — or whether you're better off buying something off the shelf.

AI Integrations

Language models, wired into the systems you already use.

Classify incoming tickets, draft customer replies, extract line items from vendor PDFs, summarize long voicemails, generate first-draft proposals. We use models from Anthropic, OpenAI, and open-weights providers depending on what the job needs — and where your data can live.

ii.
AI Dashboards

A single screen your team will actually look at.

Live numbers pulled from the places they live — not copy-pasted from six tabs. Ask the dashboard a question in plain English and get back a number and a chart. Built to match your vocabulary, not a SaaS vendor's.

Looks like

  • Sales pipeline & win rate

iii.
Company Analytics

Know why last month happened — before next month happens.

The numbers are in your systems already; they're just locked behind exports and pivot tables nobody has time for. We pull them out, connect them, and set up the questions you'd actually ask if you could. Week-over-week trends. Outliers. Customers quietly drifting.

Answers questions like

  • Why did Tuesday's labor spike?

  • What is our real cost per lead?

Workflow Automation

When a job is marked complete, the invoice drafts itself, the customer gets a thank-you note, the tech gets their next assignment, and the books close a day earlier. Custom flows in software that already exists, or a small internal tool when nothing fits.

05 Questions We Get

Straight answers, no pitch deck.

What kinds of small businesses do you work with?

Service trades, small manufacturers, clinics, logistics and dispatch operations, accounting and professional service firms, and local retailers. If your team tracks work, quotes, clients, or inventory — and the tracking is scattered across spreadsheets, email, and memory — we can probably help.

Is “AI” just ChatGPT with extra steps?

No. We use large language models where they fit — reading messy documents, drafting responses, classifying free-text fields — and we use regular software engineering for everything else. Most of the value comes from the parts that aren't AI: clean data, a real dashboard, a form that writes to the right system.

Do you work with businesses outside of Chattanooga?

Yes, but local is the focus. We prioritize engagements within about an hour of Chattanooga — Cleveland, Dayton, Dunlap, Fort Oglethorpe, Jasper, Ringgold — so we can meet in person when it matters.

How do engagements usually start?

A short conversation, usually on-site or over coffee. We look at the work that eats the most time, the reports nobody trusts, and the hand-offs where things fall through. If there's a fit, we scope a first project — typically 2 to 6 weeks — with a fixed price and a working result at the end.

Is my data safe?

Your data stays in your systems. We build integrations that run inside accounts you own and control. When we use AI models, we use providers with no-training terms on business data, and we avoid sending sensitive information to models at all when it isn't necessary.

What does this cost?

Most first projects land between $4,000 and $18,000 depending on scope. We quote fixed prices tied to specific outcomes rather than hourly retainers, and we're up front when something isn't worth building.

We already have a software vendor. Can you work alongside them?

Usually, yes. Most small businesses already have a CRM, accounting system, or field service tool. We build the connective tissue around what you have — not a replacement.
